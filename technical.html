Technical Questions

After studying so much of certain topics in computer science, I often find myself surprised when technical interviewers ask me very simple questions, as if I didn’t know the subject at all. But that is not uncommon and every candidate should embrace it and answer masterfully. I remember being asked “what is binary?”, not “what is binary used for?” or “how do computers work?", they just asked me “what is binary?”. Each interviewer has the right to come up with their own questions. However, asking great questions is a science in itself, so don’t be surprised when you are asked a bad one. Not every interviewer has mastered it, even if they have a high rank in their company or years of education, they may still not know what a great question looks like. Great technical questions can be answered well if you can quickly think creatively. Even if you can’t come up with an answer quickly, it is a good idea to inform the interviewer that you have an idea of how to solve it and ask if you can take another minute to elaborate your answer.
