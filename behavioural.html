Behavioural Questions

One of the most sought after traits of new employees is their ability to be friendly, energetic, and coachable (someone who is willing to listen and learn). Nobody likes to work with a smarty pants (someone who thinks they know everything). Behavioural questions are designed to test how a candidate responds to a variety of situations related to relationships with other coworkers. A candidate who can convincingly tell a short story about how he or she managed to break through a problematic team and get the job done by orchestrating the team more effectively will be preferred by employers.
